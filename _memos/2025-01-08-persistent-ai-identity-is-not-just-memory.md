A common assumption is that persistent identity in AI systems reduces to persistent memory. This memo argues that assumption is wrong. Memory is a precondition, not the thing itself.

The full memo will develop the distinction between memory as stored data and identity as an organizing principle — and trace the implications for how we design AI systems meant to persist.
