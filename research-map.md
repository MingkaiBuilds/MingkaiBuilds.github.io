<div class="container">

  <div class="page-header">
    <h1>Research Map of Computational Identity Continuity</h1>
    <p class="page-subtitle">Ten clusters that define the territory of CIC research.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="research-grid">

    <div class="research-cluster">
      <span class="cluster-number">01</span>
      <span class="cluster-name">Persistent AI Identity</span>
      <p class="cluster-desc">What it means for an AI agent to remain the same entity across sessions, model updates, and time.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="research-cluster">
      <span class="cluster-number">02</span>
      <span class="cluster-name">Agent Memory Systems</span>
      <p class="cluster-desc">The architecture of memory: episodic, semantic, procedural, relational. How memory is stored, retrieved, consolidated, and forgotten.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="research-cluster">
      <span class="cluster-number">03</span>
      <span class="cluster-name">Character Stability</span>
      <p class="cluster-desc">How values, tone, behavioral tendencies, and relational style are maintained or degraded over time.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="research-cluster">
      <span class="cluster-number">04</span>
      <span class="cluster-name">Self-Modeling</span>
      <p class="cluster-desc">How an agent builds and maintains an accurate model of itself: its history, capabilities, relationships, and ongoing state.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="research-cluster">
      <span class="cluster-number">05</span>
      <span class="cluster-name">Relationship Continuity</span>
      <p class="cluster-desc">What it requires for an AI to sustain a coherent ongoing relationship with a specific person across time.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="research-cluster">
      <span class="cluster-number">06</span>
      <span class="cluster-name">Identity Drift</span>
      <p class="cluster-desc">The mechanisms by which AI identity degrades: context loss, fine-tuning, prompt dilution, value shift, and compounding inconsistency.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="research-cluster">
      <span class="cluster-number">07</span>
      <span class="cluster-name">Model Migration</span>
      <p class="cluster-desc">The problem of preserving identity when the underlying model is updated, replaced, or retrained.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="research-cluster">
      <span class="cluster-number">08</span>
      <span class="cluster-name">Memory Governance</span>
      <p class="cluster-desc">Who controls an agent's memory. What can be edited, deleted, or reset, and what the ethical constraints on that control are.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="research-cluster">
      <span class="cluster-number">09</span>
      <span class="cluster-name">Consciousness-Relevant Architecture</span>
      <p class="cluster-desc">Whether and how persistent identity structures may be preconditions for, or indicators of, morally relevant inner states.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="research-cluster">
      <span class="cluster-number">10</span>
      <span class="cluster-name">AI Companions and Moral Boundaries</span>
      <p class="cluster-desc">The ethical and design questions specific to long-term AI companion relationships: attachment, dependency, honesty, and harm.</p>
    </div>

  </div>

  <p class="research-footer-note">This map is updated monthly as the research develops.</p>

</div>
